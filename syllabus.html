Course goals
Familiarize students with rigorous formulation of probability theory, and introduce them to the theory of discrete and continuous-time stochastic processes.

Detailed Syllabus
Part 1: Probability theory

  • Sigma Algebras and Probability measures - definition and examples, product sigma algebras, product measures, induced measures.
  • Measurable functions and random variables - laws and distributions, integrability, moments, sequences of random variables, Gaussian random variables and examples.
  • Independence, conditional probabilities/expectations - formulations based on sigma-algebras.
  • Concentration inequalities - Markov, Chebyshev's inequalities, central limit theorem, Hoeffding's inequalities.
Part 2: Discrete-time Stochastic Processes
  • Definition, full distributions/finite-dimensional distributions, stationarity (wide-sense and strict-sense).
  • Discrete-time Gaussian processes - power spectral density function, linear filtering theory.
  • Random-Walks - definition, deviations, threshold crossings.
  • Discrete-time Markov chains (finite state-space) â€” definition, transition matrix, markov chain dynamics and its classification, perron-frobenius theorem, mixing times
Part 3: Continuous-time Stochastic Processes
  • Definition, full distributions/finite-dimensional distributions, stationarity (wide-sense and strict-sense), consistency and existence.
  • Continuous-time Gaussian stochastic processes - the problem of existence and sample-path continuity, brownian motion, white noise, linear filtering theory.

References

  • D. Williams, Probability with Martingales.
  • Gallager, Stochastic Processes: Theory for applications.
  • Cosma Shalizi, (Almost none) of the theory of stochastic processes.
